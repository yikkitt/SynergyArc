Not All Cardboard Is Equal

"Isn't cardboard just flimsy?"

We hear this every time. And it's a fair question — because most people have only seen corrugated cardboard or thin honeycomb used in packaging. Enboard is a different category entirely. Here's how it compares.

Corrugated Board Standard Honeycomb Enboard Honeycomb
Thickness options 3–5mm Varies, often thin 5mm / 10mm / 16mm
Structural strength Low — bends easily Fragile — weak at joints High — solid and rigid
Pieces needed per item Many — layers required Many — poor load distribution Fewer — efficient construction
Print surface quality Rough, limited finish Inconsistent Smooth, Pantone-accurate print
Assembly method Glue-heavy, messy Glue or tape Slot-and-fit, no tools
Comparable product tier Budget packaging Budget display Lower range of ReBoard — more solid
Suitable for furniture & booths No No Yes
Recyclable Yes Yes Yes

The closest international benchmark for Enboard is ReBoard — a premium sustainable board used in high-end retail and exhibition globally. Enboard sits at ReBoard's entry level in terms of price, but matches or exceeds it in structural rigidity for event applications. It is a fundamentally different product from the corrugated cardboard your printer uses for shipping boxes.

16mm
The thickness difference is the whole story.

Corrugated board at 3–5mm means you need multiple layers bonded together to achieve any structural depth — more material, more labour, more failure points. Enboard comes in 5mm, 10mm, and 16mm single panels. One piece does the job that would take three corrugated layers to attempt. For furniture, counters, booth walls, and arches — that efficiency is the difference between a build that works and one that doesn't.
